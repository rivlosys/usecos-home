How Much House Can I Afford in Canada (2026 Edition)

Buying a home in Canada has become a complex puzzle of rising interest rates, strict stress tests, and evolving down payment rules. If you are asking "how much house can I afford?" you aren't just looking for a number—you're looking for a strategy to ensure you don't overextend yourself.

In 2026, affordability is determined by your "Debt Service Ratios" and the current benchmark rates set by the Bank of Canada. Let's break down exactly how lenders view your finances.

1. The Golden Rules: GDS and TDS Ratios

Canadian lenders use two primary calculations to determine your maximum mortgage amount:

  • Gross Debt Service (GDS): Your monthly housing costs (mortgage, heat, taxes, and 50% of condo fees) should not exceed 32-39% of your gross monthly income.
  • Total Debt Service (TDS): Your total monthly debt obligations (housing costs plus car loans, credit cards, and student loans) should not exceed 40-44% of your gross monthly income.

2. The Mortgage Stress Test in 2026

Even if you find a great mortgage rate, you must qualify at a higher "stress test" rate. Usually, this is either 5.25% or your contract rate plus 2%—whichever is higher. This ensures that if rates rise in the future, you can still afford your home.

For 2026, buyers should expect to be tested at rates between 6.5% and 8%, significantly reducing the "sticker price" of the home you can purchase compared to previous years.

3. Down Payment Requirements

In Canada, the minimum down payment depends on the purchase price:

  • $500,000 or less: 5% of the purchase price.
  • $500,001 to $999,999: 5% on the first $500k, and 10% on the portion above $500k.
  • $1,000,000 or more: A flat 20% down payment is required.

Remember, if you put down less than 20%, you must pay for Mortgage Default Insurance (CMHC), which is added to your mortgage balance.

Example Affordability Scenario

Imagine a household in Ontario with a combined gross annual income of $120,000:

  • Monthly Gross Income: $10,000
  • Max Housing Cost (39% GDS): $3,900/month
  • Estimated Max Purchase Price: Approximately $520,000 - $580,000 (depending on current rates and existing debt).
