What is the Mortgage Stress Test?

The stress test ensures you can still afford your mortgage payments if interest rates rise. Lenders qualify you at a higher rate (contract rate + 2% or 5.25%, whichever is greater).

Everything You Need to Know About the 2026 Mortgage Stress Test

The "Stress Test" is officially known as the OSFI B-20 Guideline. It was introduced to ensure that Canadian homeowners could still afford their monthly payments even if interest rates spiked during their mortgage term.

How the Calculation Works

Lenders do not qualify you based on the rate you actually pay (the contract rate). Instead, they use a "qualifying rate" which is the higher of:

  • Your contract interest rate plus 2%.
  • The OSFI floor rate (currently 5.25%).

For example, if you find a great 5-year fixed rate of 4.25%, the bank will test your finances as if the rate were 6.25%.

Why the Stress Test Matters for Your Budget

The stress test effectively reduces your borrowing power by about 20% to 25%. If you could technically afford a $600,000 mortgage based on your actual rate, the stress test might limit your approval to $480,000. This is the primary hurdle for first-time buyers in expensive markets like Toronto and Vancouver.

Who Needs to Pass the Test?

Virtually every borrower dealing with a federally regulated lender (the "Big 6" banks) must pass the test. This includes:

  • Insured Borrowers: Those with less than 20% down payment.
  • Uninsured Borrowers: Those with 20% or more down payment.
  • Refinancing: When you break your mortgage to take out equity or change lenders.

Strategies to Pass the Stress Test

If you are struggling to qualify for the amount you need, consider these options:

  1. Credit Unions: Some provincially regulated credit unions are not strictly bound by federal OSFI rules and may use more flexible qualifying rates.
  2. Longer Fixed Terms: Sometimes, shifting from a variable to a 5-year fixed rate can provide a slightly lower qualifying rate if the spread is favorable.
  3. Larger Down Payment: Lowering the total loan amount is the most direct way to ensure your income covers the "stressed" payment amount.
