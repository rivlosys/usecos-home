How Much House Can I Afford in Canada (2026)

Navigating the Canadian real estate market in 2026 requires more than just looking at listing prices. With fluctuating interest rates and strict lending criteria, the real question is: How much will a bank actually lend you?

Affordability is no longer just about your income; it's about your debt-to-income balance and your ability to pass the federal stress test. Here is exactly how to calculate your home buying power today.

The 32/44 Rule: Understanding Debt Ratios

Canadian lenders use two primary formulas to determine your maximum mortgage amount:

  • Gross Debt Service (GDS): This covers your housing costs (mortgage, heat, taxes). Usually, this shouldn't exceed 32% to 39% of your pre-tax income.
  • Total Debt Service (TDS): This includes housing costs plus all other debts like car loans and credit cards. Lenders generally cap this at 40% to 44%.

The 2026 Mortgage Stress Test

Even if you find a mortgage rate of 4.5%, the "Stress Test" requires you to prove you can handle payments at a much higher rate. In 2026, you must qualify at either 5.25% or your contract rate plus 2%—whichever is higher.

This rule significantly lowers your actual purchasing power, often by as much as 20%, to ensure stability if global interest rates shift.

Minimum Down Payment Requirements

In Canada, your down payment is tiered based on the purchase price of the home:

  • Up to $500,000: 5% of the purchase price.
  • $500,001 to $999,999: 5% on the first $500k, and 10% on the portion above that.
  • $1,000,000 and over: A flat 20% minimum down payment is required.

Remember that if you put down less than 20%, you will be required to purchase CMHC insurance, which adds to your total loan amount.

Example Affordability Scenario

Consider a couple in Alberta earning a combined $150,000 per year with no other major debts:

  • Max Monthly GDS: ~$4,500
  • Estimated Max Purchase Price: ~$650,000 (assuming a 10% down payment and 2026 stress test rates).

However, if that same couple has a $600 monthly payment for a truck, their buying power could drop by nearly $75,000. Before buying used, always use a VIN decoder to ensure the vehicle doesn't have hidden issues that could lead to unexpected repair costs during your first year of homeownership.
