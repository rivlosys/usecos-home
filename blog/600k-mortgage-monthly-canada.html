How much is a $600,000 mortgage per month?

For a $600,000 home with a minimum down payment, your monthly payment will typically range from $3,400 to $3,900 depending on your interest rate and amortization period.

CMHC Insurance and Monthly Costs for a $600,000 Home

Purchasing a $600,000 home in Canada triggers specific rules regarding down payments and mandatory mortgage default insurance (often called CMHC insurance). Understanding these numbers is essential for setting your monthly budget.

1. The Down Payment Requirement

In Canada, the minimum down payment for a $600,000 home is not a flat percentage. It is calculated in two tiers:

  • 5% on the first $500,000 ($25,000)
  • 10% on the remaining $100,000 ($10,000)

Therefore, your minimum down payment for a $600k house is $35,000.

2. CMHC Insurance Premiums for $600k

Because $35,000 is less than 20% of the purchase price, you must pay for mortgage insurance. For a $600k purchase with minimum down:

  • Loan Amount: $565,000
  • Insurance Rate: 4.00%
  • Total Premium: $22,600

This $22,600 is usually added to your mortgage balance, bringing your total loan to $587,600.

3. Monthly Payment Breakdown (Estimated)

Assuming a 4.5% interest rate and a 25-year amortization, here is what your monthly cash flow might look like:

  • Mortgage (Principal & Interest): ~$3,250
  • Property Taxes (Est.): ~$400
  • Heating/Utilities: ~$150
  • Total Monthly Carrying Cost: ~$3,800

How to Avoid the Insurance Premium

The only way to avoid the $20k+ insurance premium on a $600,000 home is to provide a 20% down payment ($120,000). Not only does this save you the insurance cost, but it also allows you to access 30-year amortizations, which can significantly lower your monthly payment to roughly $2,400.

What to Consider

At $600,000, your minimum down payment is $35,000 (5% on first $500k, 10% on next $100k). This puts you in the "high-ratio" category, meaning CMHC insurance will be added to your mortgage balance.
